Using AI to build a better Human: The Age of AI

  1. Home
  2. Blog 3

I start with a question, ” How might an improved human body be created using artificial intelligence, machine learning, and neural networks? What will body augmentation's ethical repercussions be? “

IIn a set out on a quest to discover the answers to five essential questions. The third episode of The Age of AI is an eight-part series. focuses on three AI applications that have the potential to completely transform society as we know it. This documentary investigates the limits of what human bodies are capable of, from prosthetic limbs that act organically and comprehend the user's intent to vision augmentation that can save lives to machine learning that enhances racecar performance.

We must cease observing AI. as something unfamiliar and ethereal — A.I. is present and will remain so. After all, human augmentation and enhancement have existed since the dawn of humanity, along with the development of the first simple tools. The only difference today is that we are pushing the boundaries of what scientists formerly believed to be feasible in all areas related to what it means to be human.

The practically infinite computational capacity available to us now has the potential to take civilization to a new level.

The Age of A.I. is an eight-episode American science documentary streaming television series narrated and hosted by American actor Robert Downey Jr. The show covers the applications of artificial intelligence (AI) in various fields, like health, robotics, space-travel, food, disaster-prevention, and others. Each 30-45 minute episode covers several different areas of AI implementation under one broader topic.
